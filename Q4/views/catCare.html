Health Care

  • Get your cat vaccinated. Recommended vaccines include:
    • Rabies Vaccine
    • Feline Panleukopenia (FPV) Vaccine
    • Feline Herpesvirus (FHV-1) Vaccine
  • Register your cat with a local Veterinarian
  • Maintain annual wellness visits with your vet
  • Remain within a healthy weight limit

Dietary Information

  • Discuss with your vet to help you choose the best diet for your cat
  • Cats need taurine, a crucial amino acid, to support heart and eye health. The food you select should be suitable for your cat or kitten's specific life stage. Properly balanced diets will include taurine.
  • Treats should make up no more than 5-10% of your cats diet
  • Avoid overfeeding by following the feeding guidelines on the cat food packaging. Adjust portions based on your cat's activity level and weight.
  • Establish a regular feeding schedule with two to three meals per day. This helps maintain their metabolism and reduces the risk of obesity.
  • Human food can be toxic or harmful to cats. Avoid feeding them chocolate, onions, garlic, alcohol, caffeine, and bones.

Grooming

  • Cats generally keep themselves clean and seldom require a bath, but regular brushing or combing is recommended. Regular grooming helps maintain a clean coat, minimizes shedding, and reduces hairball occurrences. For further information please refer to:
    • ASPCA Cat Grooming
    • The Spruce Pets

House Prep

  • Provide a clean, dry sleeping area for your pet in your home
  • Regularly wash your cat's bedding
  • Keep your cat indoors for safety and longevity.
  • Outdoor cats face risks such as:
    • Disease and infection
    • Fights with other animals
    • Getting lost
    • Injuries
